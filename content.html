Possibilities for the Project

From before Year 12, I realised that I had grown a deep interest in space and would love to revolve a project around it. Space would be perfect as there is a broad spectrum of topics to cover and is growing important for future generations.

This idea of content coupled with my broad use of many software to create different products allowed me to satisfy the requirements and needs for my project:

  1. Animated Information Idea – 3D realistic animation with text displayed as it goes to each planet/object. Would utilise Blender and Adobe After Effects.
  2. Website Showcasing the Nature of Space – An informative medium for people to engagingly learn about the characteristics/ideas of space.
  3. Website About Space with Quiz – Similar to idea #2, however with a quiz that would engage the audience more.
  4. Physical Models of the Properties of the Solar System – 3D printed objects that are seen in space (planets, asteroids, blackholes, etc.) with a text tile adjacent to display the information.

From here I was able to create concept sketches to see if these ideas would work practically and generated ways to improve upon these. With the sketches and concepts in my head, I based the decided upon the most appropriate and realistic one that I could create in order to obtain the best quality possible.

Exploration of the Need

My proposed product will be an informative website about the characteristics and uniqueness of space. It will include the topics of: The Solar System, The Unique Universe and Our Future. These topics will provide all the necessary information while still being engaging and not boring. The website will consist of text, images and videos created through many different methods to successfully engage the audience. This website will be focused towards the target market of upper primary school students and teachers.

Therefore, the need of my product is to inform the younger generations of the characteristics of space, sparking an interest in them young so that they can continue a successful future after our generation’s demise. This is an essential need as there is rapid progression in technology towards space and younger people must have a sparked interest in the topic so that the technology and innovation continues to progress.

Research of the Need - Formal

After some brief research, I figured found that the United Nations Office of Outer Space Affairs has proven that space related topics often spark students' curiosity and imagination and encourage youth of both genders to become increasingly involved in the sciences. However, the NSW curriculum only addresses that students must “identify that Earth is part of a system of planets orbiting around a star (the Sun)”. This curriculum was/is sufficient for students similar to me, and my product must address this curriculum and have an expansion of knowledge in order to successfully satisfy the need.

I then began to research children wanting to go to space and being interesting. This led to a realization that the informing children of space was a necessity as a survey conducted by LEGO found that 86% of children are interested in space exploration and 90% more want to learn more about it. Also, there is a vast want to become an astronaut throughout the world with most coming from China with 56% of children but still being in the top 5 for UK and US with 11% which is still a large number (figure 1.1). This shows that there is a legitimate need for informing children of our universe and sparking interest in younger generations so these statistics can increase

Market Research



I conducted both primary and secondary research of the Market. The secondary research has been included because there I needed to figure out how to successfully engage the target market in order for my product to be successfully. I conducted primary research to find out what the topics of the product will be and explore the basis that my products’ need must fulfill.

Secondary Research

The target market for this product is students and teachers of Years 5 and 6 of primary school. This broad audience will allow for me to access a large amount of people and spark an interest in as many children as possible so that they can develop into the necessary fields in the future. Successful engagement of the target market includes:

  1. use a mix of media (videos, drawing, photos etc)
  2. Let the students have control
  3. low modal language, sharp concise points
  4. not an information overboard (balance between text and other media).
  5. Simple navigation of website

Is this being done effectively?
Currently, along the entire spectrum of education there are many websites that successfully engage the target market of my product. However, for websites specifically for space, it is consistent that there is a confusing navigation as well as an overload of information. This causes the target audience to not be engage, thus giving a gap in the market for my product.

Exploration of a Designer

Philipp Dettmer

Philipp Dettmer is the creator of the successful Kurzgesagt which is a German Youtube channel that informs people about interesting aspects of life, focusing on the exploration of space and how unique it is. Dettmer’s vision is to make a story out of everything and ask big questions about the world and our future in order to spark interest in individuals to push for a better, more advanced future. Dettmer’s design of Kurzgesagt is similar to my MDP as he aims to inform an audience and spark an interest in individuals as well as the content of his page is very similar to mine. I also want to ask the big questions about space; however, my product will be focused more on the younger generations, unlike Dettmer’s product which is focused for more qualified people as his concepts are explained more complex.
Dettmer has assisted me in the designing of my project as he has shown me what is engaging to broad audiences, unlike other website which are aimed towards academic individuals. His use of artwork and visualisation as a medium for informing is a goal that I hope to achieve to a similar level; using many mediums to convey the project.
